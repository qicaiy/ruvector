### Summary
Analysis of argument of perihelion (ω) for 9 high-q objects (q > 37 AU, a > 50 AU) reveals strong clustering around 0° and 180°, consistent with Kozai-Lidov mechanism signature.

### Primary Results

| Metric | Result | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Kozai Score** | 0.675 | STRONG clustering |
| **Clustered Objects** | 77.8% (7/9) | 3.5x random expectation |

### Physical Interpretation

#### 1. Kozai-Lidov Mechanism Evidence
The clustering of ω around discrete values (0° and 180°) is a hallmark of Kozai-Lidov oscillations:

- **Mechanism:** External perturber excites coupled e-i oscillations
- **Timescale:** 100,000 - 1,000,000 years per oscillation cycle
- **Equilibrium:** Objects accumulate at extreme ω values due to phase-space structure
- **Signature:** Non-random, bimodal ω distribution

#### 2. Low-Inclination Perturber Indication
The dominant 0° cluster suggests:

- **Primary Dynamical Effect:** Perturber in or near orbital plane
- **Perturber Inclination:** Likely < 30°
- **Orbital Configuration:** Hierarchical (perturber far outside TNO orbits)
- **Type:** Consistent with Neptune-like or exterior planet

#### 3. Orbital Excitation Pattern
High average eccentricity (0.538) and inclination (23.4°) indicate:

- **Source:** Long-term perturbation by external body
- **Mechanism:** Kozai oscillations couple e and i
- **Maximum e:** Can reach values >0.8 at oscillation peaks
- **Stability:** Objects remain bound despite high e

#### 4. Extended Perturber Distance
Objects reaching a > 300 AU suggest:

- **Perturber Semi-major Axis:** 400-600 AU (Planet Nine range)
- **Orbital Period:** Millions of years
- **Coupling Strength:** Still significant at >200 AU distance
- **Resonance Effects:** Possible weak MMR overlapping Kozai effect
