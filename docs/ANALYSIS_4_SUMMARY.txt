KEY LIMITATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Small sample size (n=5 high-inclination objects)
   → Larger TNO survey recommended for robust statistics

2. Observational bias
   → High-i objects harder to detect (low albedo projection)
   → Sample may be incomplete

3. Age uncertainty
   → Cannot distinguish recent vs. ancient scattering for individuals
   → Requires orbital integration and age dating

4. Mass-distance degeneracy
   → Cannot uniquely determine perturber mass vs. distance alone
   → Need N-body simulations or additional constraints

5. Simplified Kozai parameter
   → Actual multi-body dynamics more complex
   → 2-body approximation ignores other planets
