## Scientific Background

### Eccentricity Pumping Mechanism

Eccentricity pumping occurs when a distant massive object (perturber) gravitationally excites TNOs through secular resonances and long-range dynamical interactions. The primary mechanisms include:

1. **Kozai-Lidov Mechanism**: Secular oscillations in eccentricity and inclination
2. **Dynamical Friction**: Drag from the primordial planetesimal disk
3. **Mean-Motion Resonances**: Orbital commensurabilities with perturber
4. **Chaotic Diffusion**: Long-timescale orbital instability

### Observational Signatures

Objects undergoing eccentricity pumping exhibit:
- High eccentricities (e > 0.7)
- Large perihelion distances (maintaining stability)
- Extended aphelion distances (reaching perturber sphere of influence)
- Clustered orbital parameters (dynamically coherent populations)
- Potential resonant relationships with perturber

## Limitations and Uncertainties

### Analysis Limitations

1. **Small Sample Size**: Only 13 objects meeting criteria
   - Statistical noise could inflate distance estimate
   - Larger sample would improve confidence

2. **Observational Bias**: Brighter objects discovered first
   - Luminosity bias → mass bias
   - May preferentially sample certain eccentricity ranges

3. **Distance Estimation Method**: Linear 3x approximation
   - Valid only for single perturber assumption
   - Multiple perturbers would complicate analysis

### Physical Uncertainties

1. **Perturber Eccentricity**: Could affect interaction strength
2. **Perturber Inclination**: Orbital plane orientation affects TNO pumping
3. **Encounter History**: May not be in quasi-static configuration
4. **Primordial Disk**: Could contribute to eccentricity excitation
