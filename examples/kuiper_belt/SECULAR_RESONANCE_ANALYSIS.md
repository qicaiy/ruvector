## 2. SECULAR PRECESSION ANALYSIS

### Precession Rate Calculations

**Definition of precession rates:**
- **Apsidal precession (ω̇):** Rate of change of perihelion argument
  - Formula: ω̇ ≈ 3·180° / √P (orbital period P in years)
  - Indicates perihelion shifting around orbit

- **Nodal precession (Ω̇):** Rate of change of ascending node
  - Formula: Ω̇ ≈ -1.5·(i/90°)·(30/a) AU⁻¹
  - Indicates orbital plane precession

### Perturbation Indicator Methodology

The **secular perturbation potential** is calculated as:

```
P = (Distance factor) + 0.5·(Eccentricity factor) + 0.3·(Inclination factor)

Where:
  Distance factor = |30 AU / a|  (inverse distance dependence)
  Eccentricity factor = e²        (high-e orbits more perturbed)
  Inclination factor = (i/90°)²   (perpendicular orbits most stable)
```

This metric identifies objects most susceptible to secular perturbations.
